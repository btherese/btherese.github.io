"I learned never to empty the well of my writing, but always to stop when there was still something there in the deep part of the well, and let it refill at night from the springs that fed it. "

-Ernest Hemmingway



Purposive communication is more than just plainly communicating with other people. It is somehow a sum of the different ways of communicating that includes writing and speaking to different audiences. 

It is a vital skill to learn. It would help us to communicate well with other people and it would help develop the communicative competence that would make us communicate effectively. It is not just important to our course, rather it is important for everybody else as well since all of us need to communicate to understand and to be understood.




Personally, communication really helped me discover more of myself. It builds a bridge allow me in connecting with other people. The more I listen, the more I understand other's perspectives. When we learn how to communicate, either using verbal or non-verbal gestures, we learn to understand the people that surrounds us every single day.

Effective communication is a way to advocate for one’s self, be better understood by others and help one get his or her thoughts and feelings across to others in a positive and safe manner. Being respectful of others’ needs with positive communication strengthens all relationships.

Effective communication should always start with yourself and how you present your ideas and thoughts without letting your emotion become a barrier in the communication process. Furthermore, this helped me in communicating with others because I became careful with how I choose the words I am going to say.

The more we communicate with other people, the more we learn about ourselves. When we continue to communicate, you can actually improve  your communication skills. These communication skills is very important as we grew older because we will need this in our future work, as teachers.
